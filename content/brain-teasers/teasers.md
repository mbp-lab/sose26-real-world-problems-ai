# Brain teasers

:::{dropdown} Bias ≠ Bias ≠ Bias

Sometimes the same term may mean different things even in the same field. Consider bias:

- **(Inductive) bias**: the assumptions a learning algorithm makes about the underlying function it is trying to learn. A model with a stricter assumptions has higher inductive bias, while a model with less assumptions has a lower inductive bias. 
**Example**: Linear regression assumes relationships are approximately linear. 

- **(Statistical) bias**:  systematic error in a model's predictions (the "bias" in the bias–variance tradeoff).
**Example**: A simple model underfits the data.

- **(Fairness) bias**: undesirable systematic disparities in how a model treats different groups or individuals. 
**Example**: A hiring model that consistently ranks equally qualified women lower than men.

**The brain teaser** 
- Can you think of cases with high inductive bias but low statistical bias? 
- Low inductive bias, low statistical bias but high fairness bias?

:::
